## Notes

- **Pork shoulder:** start the overnight brine **Friday evening May 22**. Roast Saturday morning — 3-hour cook at 275°F means plan for a late-afternoon or early-evening dinner.
- **Chicken:** buy 2½ lb total chicken breasts; slice 1½ lb thin for fajitas (Mon) and cut 1 lb into nugget-sized pieces for sticky wings (Thu). Keep separate in fridge until use.
- **Ginger:** one 3–4 inch piece of fresh ginger covers both the sticky wings (1 tbsp grated) and the pork brine (1 tbsp, can grate instead of buying separate ginger paste).
- **Apple cider vinegar:** one bottle covers both the pork brine (¼ cup) and salmon salad dressing (2 tbsp).
- **Herbs for Turkish eggs:** the recipe calls for a cup of mixed tender herbs — cilantro (already buying), parsley, basil (already buying), and dill. A small bunch of parsley and dill is all that's needed.
- **Buttermilk:** if you can't find a small carton, add 1 tbsp white vinegar or lemon juice to ¾ cup whole milk and let sit 5 minutes — works the same in the wing dredge.
- **Juniper berries:** found in the spice aisle (McCormick carries them) or a specialty grocer. Can omit from the pork brine if unavailable; the brine will still work well with the other aromatics.
