## Notes

- **Pesto math:** one standard 7 oz jar ≈ 14 tbsp. Soup needs ½ cup (8 tbsp) + salmon dressing needs ⅓ cup (5½ tbsp) = 13½ tbsp total. One jar is barely enough — buy a 10 oz jar if available.
- **Brine timing:** start the pork brine **Friday evening (May 1)** — minimum 8 hours before Saturday morning roasting.
- **Chicken:** 2 lbs total covers Monday soup (1 lb) and Thursday wings (1 lb). Store them together, but portion separately before cooking.
- **Fresh herbs:** thyme and rosemary overlap between soup (Monday) and pork rub (Saturday) — one bunch of each covers both nights.
- **Juniper berries** can be found in the spice aisle of specialty grocery stores (Whole Foods, World Market). Substitute: ½ tsp black pepper + ½ tsp allspice if unavailable.
- **Buttermilk substitute:** combine ¾ cup whole milk + 1 tbsp apple cider vinegar; let sit 5 minutes. Saves buying a full carton.
- **Ginger:** wings need 1 tbsp grated fresh; pork brine needs 1 tbsp ginger paste. One fresh knob covers both — grate directly for each.
- **Heart-health mods to remember at cooking time:**
  - Salmon salad (Tue): halve bacon (4 strips), Greek yogurt for mayo in dressing, skip buttered ciabatta
  - Turkish eggs (Wed): 2 whole eggs + 2 whites, reduce feta to 3 oz, chile butter on the side for Alisa
  - Pesto soup (Mon): swap 2 tbsp butter for olive oil
