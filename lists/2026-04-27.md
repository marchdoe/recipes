## Notes

- **Pesto:** only salmon salad needs pesto this week (⅓ cup / ~5 tbsp). A standard 7 oz jar is more than enough — no need to buy large.
- **Brine timing:** start the pork brine **Friday evening (May 1)** — minimum 8 hours before Saturday morning roasting.
- **Chicken:** 2½ lbs total covers Monday fajitas (1½ lb) and Thursday wings (1 lb). Buy as one package and portion before cooking.
- **Fresh herbs for pork rub:** rosemary, sage, and thyme are used together in the Saturday rub only (no overlap with other nights this week). Small pre-packed bunches work fine.
- **Ginger:** wings need 1 tbsp grated fresh ginger; pork brine uses 1 tbsp jarred ginger paste. Buy both — fresh knob for Thursday, ginger paste jar for Saturday.
- **Juniper berries** are in the spice aisle at Whole Foods or World Market. If unavailable, substitute ½ tsp black pepper + ½ tsp allspice in the brine.
- **Buttermilk substitute:** combine ¾ cup whole milk + 1 tbsp apple cider vinegar; let sit 5 minutes. Saves buying a full carton.
- **Cotija/feta for fajitas:** recipe calls for ½ cup — with the heart-health mod, reduce to ¼ cup or skip. The same block you buy for Turkish eggs covers both nights.
- **Heart-health mods to remember at cooking time:**
  - Fajitas (Mon): olive oil instead of butter; all Greek yogurt in sauce (no mayo); reduce/skip cotija; use corn tortillas
  - Salmon salad (Tue): 4 strips bacon (not 8); Greek yogurt instead of mayo in dressing; skip buttered ciabatta
  - Turkish eggs (Wed): 2 whole eggs + 4 whites; reduce feta to 3 oz; halve the chile butter; serve chile butter on the side for Alisa
  - Asian wings (Thu): use low-sodium soy sauce as specified; pair with plain rice or cucumbers to offset sodium
