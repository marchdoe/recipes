## Ingredients

### Main dish
- 1½ pounds boneless chicken breasts, thinly sliced
- 3 tablespoons salted butter or olive oil
- 3 cloves garlic, chopped
- 3–4 tablespoons taco seasoning (homemade recipe in source notes)
- Salt and black pepper, to taste
- 1 yellow onion, sliced
- 2 bell peppers, sliced
- 1 poblano pepper, sliced
- 8–10 flour tortillas, warmed
- Avocado, limes, and cilantro, for serving

### Cotija verde sauce
- ½ cup plain Greek yogurt
- ¼ cup mayo or additional Greek yogurt
- ¾ cup salsa verde
- 1–3 tablespoons pickled jalapeños, chopped
- ½ cup chopped cilantro
- ½ cup crumbled cotija or feta cheese

## Directions

1. Preheat oven to 425°F.
2. On a baking sheet, combine chicken with butter/oil, garlic, taco seasoning, salt, and pepper.
3. Add bell peppers, poblano pepper, and onions; season with salt and pepper. Spread evenly and bake 20–25 minutes until chicken cooks through and peppers begin charring.
4. Mix sauce ingredients in a bowl; season with salt.
5. Assemble fajitas in warmed tortillas with chicken, peppers, onions, avocado, sauce, cilantro, and cheese.

## Notes

- **Heart-health tweaks for Doug:**
  - Use olive oil instead of butter (cuts saturated fat)
  - Use all Greek yogurt for the sauce (skip mayo entirely)
  - Reduce or skip the cotija/feta (cheese is the main saturated-fat source here)
  - Use whole-corn tortillas instead of flour for more fiber
- **Leftover-friendly:** chicken + peppers reheat well for next-day lunch (assemble fresh tortillas at lunchtime)
- **Kid-friendly:** spice level adjusts via taco seasoning and pickled jalapeño quantity in the sauce
- **6 servings is generous for two adults** — plan for 2 dinners + a lunch
