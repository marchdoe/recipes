## Ingredients

### Whipped feta
- 6 ounces feta cheese
- ¼ cup plain Greek yogurt

### Eggs, greens, and chile
- ⅓ cup extra virgin olive oil
- 4 large eggs
- 1 clove garlic, minced or grated
- 4 cups mixed baby spinach and arugula
- Kosher salt and black pepper, to taste
- Zest and juice from 1 lemon
- 1 cup tender herbs (cilantro, parsley, basil, dill)
- 1 Persian cucumber, thinly sliced
- 2 tablespoons salted butter
- 1 teaspoon crushed red pepper flakes
- ½ teaspoon paprika
- 1 avocado, sliced
- 2 tablespoons everything bagel spice

## Directions

1. Combine feta and Greek yogurt in food processor. Pulse until smooth and creamy, about 3 minutes. Add pepper to taste. Refrigerate up to 3 days.
2. Heat 3 tablespoons olive oil in a medium skillet over medium-high heat. When oil shimmers, add eggs and fry to desired doneness. Transfer to plate.
3. In same skillet, add 1 tablespoon oil, garlic, and greens. Season with salt and pepper. Cook 2–3 minutes until wilted. Remove from heat. Toss with lemon zest, juice, herbs, and cucumber.
4. In small saucepan, melt butter with 2 tablespoons olive oil, red pepper flakes, and paprika.
5. Spread whipped feta into serving bowls. Top with eggs, greens, and avocado. Sprinkle avocado with everything spice. Drizzle chile butter over eggs.

## Notes

- **Heart-health note for Doug:** eggs (yolks contain cholesterol — about 200mg per yolk), feta and butter raise saturated fat. Mitigations:
  - Use 2 whole eggs + 4 whites to cut cholesterol significantly
  - Reduce feta to 3 oz; use lower-fat feta
  - Halve the chile butter
- **Category is "breakfast"** but this works well as breakfast-for-dinner on a Friday or Saturday night.
