## Ingredients

### Chicken
- 2 tablespoons extra virgin olive oil
- 1½ pounds boneless skinless chicken breasts or thighs, cubed
- 1 tablespoon chipotle chili powder
- 2 teaspoons smoked paprika
- 1 teaspoon garlic powder
- 1 teaspoon onion powder
- 2 teaspoons honey
- Chili flakes, salt, and black pepper, to taste

### Salad
- 4–5 cups shredded kale and/or romaine, mixed
- 2 Persian cucumbers, chopped
- ½ cup oil-packed sun-dried tomatoes, sliced
- 1 avocado, diced or sliced
- 6 ounces crumbled feta cheese
- 1 cup mixed herbs (basil, dill, parsley)
- 1–2 cups tzatziki sauce
- 2 tablespoons sesame seeds

### Tahini dressing
- ½ cup tahini
- 1 clove garlic, grated
- 3 tablespoons toasted sesame oil
- 2 tablespoons lemon or orange juice
- 1 tablespoon rice vinegar
- 1 teaspoon honey

## Directions

1. Preheat oven to 425°F.
2. On a baking sheet, combine chicken, olive oil, chili powder, paprika, garlic powder, onion powder, honey, and seasonings. Toss thoroughly and roast 15–20 minutes until lightly charred.
3. Add kale/romaine, cucumbers, and sun-dried tomatoes to a large salad bowl.
4. Prepare dressing by combining all ingredients in a blender or jar; blend until smooth. Thin with ¼–½ cup water. Adjust seasoning to taste.
5. Dress salad greens, massaging dressing into the kale. Top with chicken, avocado, feta, herbs, and sesame seeds. Add generous amounts of tzatziki and extra dressing; toss lightly and serve.

## Notes

- **Heart-health note:** chicken + kale + tahini are good; 6 oz of feta adds notable saturated fat and sodium. Mitigations:
  - Reduce feta to 3 oz; use lower-fat feta
  - Use store tzatziki sparingly (or make at home with low-fat Greek yogurt)
- **Greek-inspired:** strong Mediterranean profile aligns with Doug's preferences.
- **6 servings:** generous for two — the chicken keeps well separately for next-day grain bowls.
