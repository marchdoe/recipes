## Ingredients

- 6 boneless chicken breasts or thighs
- Salt and black pepper
- 1 tablespoon all-purpose or gluten-free flour
- 8 slices prosciutto
- 4 tablespoons salted butter
- 2 shallots, chopped
- 4 cloves garlic, chopped
- 2 tablespoons fresh thyme
- 6 fresh sage leaves
- 1 cup dry orzo pasta
- 2 cups cherry or Sungold tomatoes
- Chili flakes, to taste
- ½ cup dry white wine
- ½ cup heavy cream or whole milk
- ½ cup grated parmesan cheese
- Fresh basil, for serving

## Directions

1. Preheat the oven to 400°F.
2. Season the chicken with salt and pepper, then lightly coat with flour.
3. Heat a large oven-safe skillet over medium-high heat. Cook the prosciutto until crisp, then remove and set aside.
4. In the same skillet, add 3 tablespoons of butter. Add the chicken, shallots, garlic, and thyme. Sear the chicken until golden, about 3–5 minutes per side.
5. Add the remaining 1 tablespoon butter, the sage, orzo, 1½ cups of the tomatoes, and season with chili flakes. Cook for 2 minutes, until the orzo is lightly toasted.
6. Pour in the wine and 2 cups water (or chicken broth). Bring to a boil and cook for 3–5 minutes. Stir in the cream and parmesan. Transfer the skillet to the oven and bake, uncovered, for 10 minutes, until the sauce is bubbling and the chicken is cooked through. Top with the remaining ½ cup fresh tomatoes, basil, more thyme, and the crispy prosciutto.

## Notes

- **Heart-health tweaks for Doug** (this recipe is rich as written — heavy cream, 4 tbsp butter, parmesan, prosciutto all push saturated fat and sodium):
  - Use **whole milk instead of heavy cream** (cuts ~10g saturated fat per ½ cup)
  - **Skip the prosciutto** or substitute Canadian bacon (prosciutto adds significant sodium + saturated fat for what is essentially a garnish)
  - Use **2 tbsp olive oil + 2 tbsp butter** instead of 4 tbsp all-butter (or all olive oil for the searing step)
  - Reduce parmesan to ¼ cup; the dish doesn't need more
  - Use **whole-grain or chickpea orzo** for fiber (chickpea orzo also bumps protein and lowers refined-carb load)
- **Boneless skinless chicken breasts** beat thighs here for cholesterol/saturated-fat — the recipe accommodates either
