## Ingredients

### Fish tacos
- 1½ pounds white fish, cut into 1-inch chunks (mahi mahi recommended)
- 1 teaspoon chili powder
- 1½ cups flour
- 1 teaspoon baking powder
- 1 cup light beer
- Vegetable or canola oil for frying
- Soft flour or corn tortillas, warmed
- Shredded cheddar or cotija cheese
- Shredded lettuce, fresh cilantro, and yogurt for serving

### Chipotle mango salsa
- 1 ripe mango, diced
- 1 ripe but firm avocado, diced
- 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
- 1–2 chipotle peppers in adobo, chopped
- Juice from 1 lime
- ¼ cup fresh cilantro, chopped
- Salt, to taste

### Creamy tomatillo sauce
- 6 tomatillos, skins removed
- 2 jalapeños, halved and seeded (optional)
- 1 cup fresh cilantro
- ¼ cup plain Greek yogurt
- ½ ripe avocado
- Juice from 1 lime
- Salt, to taste

## Directions

### Fish tacos
1. Heat 1–2 inches of oil in a medium pot over medium heat until a deep-fry thermometer reaches 375°F.
2. Season fish with chili powder. Combine flour, baking powder, and beer in a bowl; stir until just combined.
3. Once oil is hot, dip fish in batter, then fry in batches 2–3 minutes until golden and cooked through. Transfer to paper towels.
4. Season with salt and lime zest.
5. Divide fish among warm tortillas; top with lettuce, salsa, and cheese.

### Chipotle mango salsa
Combine all ingredients in a bowl. Taste and adjust spiciness and salt as needed.

### Creamy tomatillo sauce
1. Preheat broiler to high. Broil peeled tomatillos for 3–5 minutes, turning occasionally, until charred.
2. Cool slightly, then blend with remaining ingredients until smooth. Adjust salt. Keeps refrigerated for up to 1 week.

## Notes

- **Heart-health caveat:** Doug's preferences explicitly limit fried foods — beer-battered deep-fried fish goes against that goal. Mitigations:
  - **Pan-sear or grill the fish** instead of deep-frying (skip flour/beer batter; just season with chili powder and grill 3–4 min/side)
  - Skip cheese, or use light Cotija
  - Use whole-corn tortillas
- **The salsas are healthy** — keep them as-is; they're the best part anyway.
- **Tomatillo sauce keeps a week** — make extra for other meals.
- 25 minutes total makes this a quick weeknight option (especially if you grill instead of fry).
