## Ingredients

### Chicken & Corn
- 1 lb boneless, skinless chicken breasts
- 1 teaspoon olive oil
- 2 cups frozen corn (defrosted and patted dry)

### Spice Rub
- 1 teaspoon chili powder
- ½ teaspoon cumin
- ½ teaspoon garlic powder
- Dash salt and pepper

### Sauce
- ½ cup light sour cream
- ¼ cup low-fat mayonnaise
- 1 teaspoon minced garlic
- ¼ teaspoon chili powder
- ⅛ teaspoon salt
- 1 tablespoon fresh lime juice
- 1 teaspoon lime zest

### Toppings
- ⅓ cup crumbled light feta cheese
- ¼ cup chopped fresh cilantro
- ¼ cup red onion, chopped
- ½ teaspoon crushed red pepper

### Optional Garnish
- 4 lime wedges
- 1½ tablespoons light Parmesan cheese

## Directions

1. Mix rub ingredients (chili powder, cumin, garlic powder, salt, pepper) together.
2. Rub chicken pieces on both sides with the spice mixture.
3. Heat 1 teaspoon oil in a cast iron skillet over medium-high heat. Brown chicken 3–5 minutes per side. Reduce to medium-low and cook until internal temperature reaches 165°F.
4. While chicken cooks, whisk sauce ingredients (sour cream, mayo, garlic, chili powder, salt, lime juice, zest) in a medium bowl.
5. Remove cooked chicken to a plate and cover to keep warm.
6. In the same unwashed pan, spread defrosted corn and let sit undisturbed 2–3 minutes until golden-brown and charred.
7. Stir corn and cook another 2 minutes.
8. Turn heat to LOW. Pour sauce over charred corn and stir gently to combine.
9. Nestle chicken back into the pan, spooning corn mixture over top. Let warm through 1 minute (do not boil).
10. Remove from heat. Sprinkle with feta, red onion, cilantro, and crushed red pepper.
11. Serve hot with lime wedges and optional Parmesan.

## Notes

- **Heart-health tweaks for Doug:** (1) Skip or halve the feta and Parmesan — both add saturated fat and sodium; the dish is punchy enough without them. (2) Swap the mayo for plain non-fat Greek yogurt — same creamy texture, cuts saturated fat and cholesterol. (3) The 388 mg sodium/serving is already modest; just watch the added salt in the rub and sauce if you're stacking sodium across the day.
