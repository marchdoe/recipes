## Ingredients

- 6 oz Pad Thai / wide rice noodles
- ⅓ cup tamari or soy sauce
- ¼ cup Thai chili sauce
- 2 tablespoons fish sauce
- 2 tablespoons rice vinegar
- 1 tablespoon lemongrass paste (optional)
- Black pepper and chili flakes, to taste
- 3 tablespoons sesame oil (or olive oil)
- 1 lb flank steak, thinly sliced
- 2 bell peppers, thinly sliced
- 2 tablespoons butter, sliced
- 1 shallot, sliced
- 4–6 cloves garlic, chopped
- 1 tablespoon chopped pickled ginger
- ½ cup chopped cilantro or Thai basil
- 4 green onions, chopped
- ¼ cup sesame seeds
- ⅓ cup roasted peanuts, chopped

## Directions

1. Soak rice noodles per package directions; drain after 6 minutes.
2. Combine tamari, Thai chili sauce, fish sauce, rice vinegar, lemongrass, black pepper, and chili flakes in a bowl.
3. Heat oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Cook beef and peppers until crispy (about 5 minutes). Add 1 tbsp butter, shallot, 3 cloves garlic, ginger, and chili flakes. Cook until garlic turns golden (1–2 minutes).
4. Add noodles and sauce; toss and cook until warmed through and sauce coats noodles (about 1 minute). Remove from heat. Stir in cilantro/basil and green onions.
5. In a small skillet, heat 1 tbsp butter, 1 clove garlic, and sesame seeds until toasted (5 minutes).
6. Serve noodles topped with green onions, peanuts, and garlicky sesame seeds. Add lime juice.
