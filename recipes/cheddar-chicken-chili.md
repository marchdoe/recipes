## Ingredients

- 4 tablespoons salted butter
- 1.5 pounds chicken breasts
- 2 yellow onions, chopped
- 1 cup chopped carrots
- 1 cup chopped celery
- 1 red pepper, chopped
- 1 poblano pepper, chopped
- 2 cans Ro-Tel tomatoes and green chilies
- 1 can tomato paste
- 1 tablespoon chopped chipotle peppers in adobo
- 2 tablespoons chili powder
- 1 tablespoon smoked paprika
- 1 tablespoon garlic powder
- 2 teaspoons ground cumin
- 1 teaspoon dried oregano
- 3–4 cups broth
- 1 can kidney beans or black beans (with liquid)
- 1 cup shredded cheddar cheese

### Toppings (any combination)
- Greek yogurt or sour cream, cilantro, avocado, green onions, jalapeños, tortilla chips, lime wedges, hot sauce, pickled red onions

## Directions

1. Combine butter, chicken, onion, carrots, celery, peppers, Ro-Tel tomatoes, tomato paste, chipotle, garlic powder, chili powder, smoked paprika, cumin, and oregano in a large Dutch oven. Heat over medium-high, stirring occasionally for about 10 minutes until vegetables soften.
2. Pour in broth and add beans with their liquid. Season with salt and pepper. Cover and simmer 10 minutes.
3. Remove chicken and shred using two forks. Return to pot and stir in cheese until melted.
4. Ladle into bowls and top as desired.

## Notes

- **Heart-health caveat:** the 4 tbsp butter + 1 cup cheddar push saturated fat up. To make it more heart-healthy:
  - Reduce butter to 1–2 tbsp (chicken cooks fine in less)
  - Use ½ cup cheese, or sub for a sprinkle on top instead of stirred in
  - Use Greek yogurt instead of sour cream for topping
- **Big batch:** 6 servings — covers two adults for dinner + 2 days of lunch leftovers.
