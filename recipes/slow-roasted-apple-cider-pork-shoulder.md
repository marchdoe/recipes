## Ingredients

### Brine
- 6 cups apple cider
- 1/4 cup apple cider vinegar
- 1/4 cup packed brown sugar
- 6 tablespoons kosher salt
- 1 teaspoon juniper berries, crushed
- 6 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 tablespoon ginger paste
- 2 cinnamon sticks
- 3 bay leaves

### Roast
- 1 (3–4 pound) boneless pork shoulder roast
- 2–3 tablespoons olive oil
- 2 medium honeycrisp apples, cored and cut into wedges
- 1 medium onion, halved and sliced

### Herb rub
- 1 cup apple cider
- 2 sprigs fresh rosemary, leaves stripped and minced
- 6 large sage leaves, minced
- 1 tablespoon thyme leaves, minced
- 1 teaspoon kosher salt
- 4 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 tablespoon maple syrup

## Directions

1. Combine all brine ingredients in a large bowl until well mixed.
2. Place pork shoulder in a brining bag or large pot; pour brine over, ensuring full submersion. Refrigerate overnight (~8 hours).
3. Preheat oven to 275°F.
4. Bring 1 cup apple cider to a boil in a small saucepan; reduce by half and set aside.
5. Mix all rub ingredients in a small bowl and set aside.
6. Heat a large heavy-bottomed skillet or cast iron pan over medium-high heat.
7. Remove pork from brine and pat dry with paper towels. Rub all sides with olive oil.
8. Sear shoulder 4 minutes per side in the hot pan.
9. Spread apples and onion in a roasting pan; place browned pork on a roasting rack on top.
10. Deglaze the searing pan with 1/4 cup apple cider; pour over the apples and onions.
11. Rub herb mixture over the pork shoulder; drizzle reduced apple cider on top.
12. Roast for 3 hours until thickest portion reaches 145°F internal temperature. Rest 5 minutes.
13. Slice and serve with the roasted apples and onions.
